How Optical Sound Works

35mm film has an optical soundtrack encoded as a narrow strip along the left edge, between the sprocket holes and the picture area.

The varying width of the transparent area in this strip encodes sound waves. In a theater projector:

  • A light source shines through the moving film
  • The varying transparency modulates the light intensity
  • A photocell converts this to an electrical signal
  • The signal is amplified and sent to speakers

This app photographs the track and digitally measures those width variations to reconstruct the audio.

Note: One frame contains only 1/24 second of audio, so the result is a brief sound burst rather than recognizable audio. But it's real sound data from real film!
